The NeMo Microservices Ecosystem

The NCP-AAI exam heavily emphasizes knowing WHAT to use WHEN. Learn the distinct functions of Curator, Customizer, Evaluator, and Retriever.

Table of Contents
  • 1 NeMo Curator (Data Prep)
  • 2 NeMo Customizer (Fine-Tuning)
  • 3 NeMo Evaluator (Benchmarking)
  • 4 NeMo Retriever (RAG Pipeline)
  • 5 Exam Relevance (The "Which Service" Question)

1NeMo Curator (Data Pre-Processing)

Before you can fine-tune a model, you need massive amounts of clean data. NeMo Curator runs on GPUs to rapidly deduplicate, clean, translate, and blend petabytes of raw text data. If a scenario involves "cleaning Wikipedia scrapes before training", the answer is Curator.

2NeMo Customizer (PEFT Fine-Tuning)

NeMo Customizer handles the actual math of fine-tuning (LoRA, P-Tuning, Full SFT, DPO). It manages spreading the LLM weights across multiple GPUs using Tensor Parallelism or Pipeline Parallelism.

3NeMo Evaluator (Benchmarking)

How do you know if Customizer actually worked? NeMo Evaluator automatically runs standard benchmarks (MMLU, HumanEval) or custom RAGAS (RAG Assessment) metrics on the tuned model to generate scorecard reports.

4NeMo Retriever (RAG Pipeline)

If you don't want to fine-tune, you use NeMo Retriever. Retriever connects the LLM to enterprise documents. It provides optimized embedding microservices and high-throughput cross-encoder re-ranking algorithms.

5Exam Relevance

Exam Trap: "You need to measure how often your new Customer Service Agent hallucinates responses that aren't in the employee handbook. Which microservice automates this?"

Answer: NeMo Evaluator. Customizer builds the model, Guardrails blocks the hallucinations at runtime, but Evaluator measures the frequency via benchmarks.
