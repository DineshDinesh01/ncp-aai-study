Tree of Thought & MCTS Algorithms

Standard prompt engineering limits models to linear thinking. Learn how advanced search algorithms like Tree of Thought (ToT) allow agents to explore alternative reasoning paths simultaneously.

1Chain vs Tree

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) forces an LLM to output a sequence of reasoning steps (A → B → C). This is highly effective but suffers from fatal flaw error cascading: if the LLM makes a mistake on step A, steps B and C represent wasted compute down a broken path.

Tree-of-Thought (ToT) allows the LLM to generate multiple alternative possibilities at step A, evaluate them locally, discard the weak ones (pruning), and continue investigating the most promising branches. It is essentially BFS or DFS search logic applied to LLM reasoning.

2Tree of Thought (ToT) Implementation

Implementing ToT requires four distinct functions inside your agent runner:

  • Decomposer: Breaks the large problem into smaller thought steps.
  • Generator: Generates *k* distinct thoughts for the current step.
  • Evaluator: Scored the generated thoughts (often by asking the LLM itself or an external tool).
  • Search Algorithm: Decide whether to pursue depth-first (DFS) or breadth-first (BFS).

You will significantly increase token usage, but the reliability in mathematical or logic puzzles skyrockets.

3Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS)

Extending ToT, MCTS borrows from reinforcement learning (famous for powering AlphaGo). It does not just score the immediate thought step; it dynamically simulates random "actions" downwards to the end of the tree, and backpropogates a win/loss score back up to the current branch.

This balances Exploitation (following known good paths) with Exploration (trying wild new steps to see if they yield better results).

4Graph of Thought (GoT)

The next iteration beyond ToT. Graph of Thought recognizes that reasoning is rarely a pure tree. Sometimes, two distinct branches arrive at identical logical conclusions. GoT allows paths to merge back together, reducing redundant search queries.

5Exam Question Targets

Exam Trap: You must know the tradeoff between ToT, CoT, and standard prompting in terms of Latency / Token Cost vs Accuracy.

ToT is vastly superior to CoT in reliability, but is extremely slow and expensive for real-time customer chatbots. It is best used for complex backend data extraction and long-running research tasks.
